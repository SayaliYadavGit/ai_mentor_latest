Introduction to currency trading or Forex trading

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Q: What Forex products does Hantec offer?

Forex trading, also known as foreign exchange trading, currency trading, or FX market, refers to the buying and selling of currencies in the forex market. The forex market is a decentralized market that operates 24 hours a day, five days a week, and is the largest financial market in the world, with a daily trading volume of over $7 trillion. In forex trading, individuals and organizations buy and sell different currency pairs, such as the US dollar and the euro, in order to make a profit. Forex trades are conducted through a broker, who acts as a mediator between buyers and sellers. A currency’s value constantly fluctuates, and forex traders take advantage of these changes by buying low and selling high or by selling high and buying low. Forex trading can be a profitable venture for those who have a solid understanding of the currency market and the factors that drive currency prices.

History of Foreign exchange market

The foreign exchange market, also known as the forex market, is a decentralized market where the buying and selling of currencies worldwide takes place, with currency pairs being the primary units of trade. The history of the forex markets can be traced back to ancient times when money was first used as a medium of exchange for goods and services.

Modern currency markets began to take shape during the 19th century when countries began to adopt the gold standard, and the exchange of foreign currencies became more widespread. In the late 1800s and early 1900s, currency exchange was mainly conducted through banks and other financial institutions, which acted as intermediaries between buyers and sellers of currencies.

History of Foreign exchange market

The foreign exchange market, also known as the forex market, is a decentralized market where the buying and selling of currencies worldwide takes place, with currency pairs being the primary units of trade. The history of the forex markets can be traced back to ancient times when money was first used as a medium of exchange for goods and services.

Modern currency markets began to take shape during the 19th century when countries began to adopt the gold standard, and the exchange of foreign currencies became more widespread. In the late 1800s and early 1900s, currency exchange was mainly conducted through banks and other financial institutions, which acted as intermediaries between buyers and sellers of currencies.

During the 20th century, the foreign exchange market saw significant growth and development. In the aftermath of World War II, the Bretton Woods Agreement was signed, which established the U.S. dollar as the dominant global currency and pegged other currencies to it. This led to the creation of the world's first modern forex market.

In the 1970s, the Bretton Woods Agreement was abandoned, and the world's currencies began to float freely against each other, leading to the ability to trade currencies freely. This marked the beginning of the modern foreign exchange market as we know it today. Since then, the forex market has become one of the world's largest and most actively traded financial markets, with a daily trading volume of over $7 trillion.

How does currency trading work?

To begin trading currencies, one must first establish a Forex trading account with a broker. The trader selects a currency pair to trade, such as the EUR/USD, and then determines whether to buy or sell the base currency (EUR) relative to the quote currency (USD) based on their market analysis and projections for the currency's value.

If the trader believes that the value of the base currency (EUR) will increase compared to the quote currency (USD), they will carry out a buy transaction for the EUR/USD currency pair. If their analysis proves to be accurate and the value of the base currency (EUR) does indeed rise, they can then execute a sell transaction for the pair and secure a profit from their currency trades.

Major players in the forex markets

Here are some of the major players in the forex market:

Central Banks – Central banks play a crucial role in the forex market as they control the supply and demand of their country’s currency. The Federal Reserve, European Central Bank, Bank of Japan, and Bank of England are some of the most influential central banks in the forex market.
Commercial Banks – Commercial banks are responsible for facilitating international trade by offering currency exchange services to their clients. They also play an important role in the forex market as they use currency hedging strategies to minimize their exposure to currency risk.
Investment Banks – Investment banks provide trading services for large institutions and corporations. They also engage in speculative trading in the forex market and offer currency derivatives products such as currency swaps and options.
Hedge Funds – Hedge funds use a variety of investment strategies to generate returns, and many of them actively trade in the forex market. They use leverage to maximize their returns and can significantly impact currency prices.
Retail Traders – Retail traders, also known as individual traders, make up a small portion of the total forex market participants. They trade through online platforms offered by retail forex brokers.
Non-Banking Financial Institutions – Non-banking financial institutions, such as insurance companies and pension funds, also participate in the forex market to manage their currency exposures.

The 6 most traded currency pairs in the world

There are several currency pairs traded in the forex market, but the most commonly traded ones are known as major currency pairs. These currency pairs make up more than 85% of the total daily forex market turnover. The following are the major currency pairs in the forex market:

EUR/USD (Euro/US Dollar) – This is the most traded currency pair in the forex market, accounting for 28% of the total daily forex market turnover. The pair represents the value of the Euro expressed in US dollars.
USD/JPY (US Dollar/Japanese Yen) – This currency pair represents the value of the US dollar expressed in the Japanese yen. It is the second most traded currency pair in the forex market, accounting for 13% of the total daily forex market turnover.
GBP/USD (British Pound/US Dollar) – This currency pair represents the value of the British pound expressed in US dollars. It is known as “The Cable” and is the third most traded currency pair in the forex market, accounting for 11% of the total daily forex market turnover.
USD/CHF (US Dollar/Swiss Franc) – This currency pair represents the value of the US dollar expressed in Swiss francs. It is the fourth most traded currency pair in the forex market, accounting for 7% of the total daily forex market turnover.
AUD/USD (Australian Dollar/US Dollar) – This currency pair represents the value of the Australian dollar expressed in US dollars. It is the fifth most traded currency pair in the forex market, accounting for 6% of the total daily forex market turnover.
USD/CAD (US Dollar/Canadian Dollar) – This currency pair represents the value of the US dollar expressed in Canadian dollars. It is the sixth most traded currency pair in the forex market, accounting for 5% of the total daily forex market turnover.

What moves the Forex market?

Supply and demand play a crucial role in determining the exchange rates of different currencies. When there is a higher demand for a particular currency, its value increases, while a decrease in demand leads to a decrease in value. On the other hand, an increase in the supply of a currency leads to a decrease in its value, while a decrease in supply causes its value to increase.

Interest rates also play a crucial role in the forex market. Central banks of different countries regularly adjust their interest rates to control inflation and maintain economic stability. These changes in interest rates can affect the demand for a particular currency and, in turn, its exchange rate.

The forex markets also play an important role in the movement of exchange rates. The foreign exchange market is vast, with participants from all over the world, and the flow of money between countries can affect the exchange rates. In addition, the forex market is highly liquid, meaning that it can respond quickly to changes in demand, which can lead to significant fluctuations in exchange rates.

Developing a trading strategy in Forex market

Developing a successful trading strategy in the forex market requires careful planning and a thorough understanding of the market. The following steps can help you create an effective strategy for trading in the forex market:

Determine your investment goals: Your investment goals will influence the type of strategy you develop. For example, if you want to generate steady profits, a more conservative strategy might be appropriate, while if you are looking to maximize profits in a short period of time, a more aggressive strategy may be called for.
Study the market: Before you start trading, it is essential to gain a comprehensive understanding of the forex market, including market participants, economic indicators, and current trends. This will help you make informed trading decisions and develop a more effective strategy.
Identify your trading style: There are different styles of trading in the forex market, including scalping, swing trading, and position trading. Identifying your preferred style will help you develop a strategy that is tailored to your individual strengths and weaknesses.
Choose your trading instruments: You can trade a wide range of currency pairs in the forex market. It’s important to choose the pairs that you are most comfortable trading and that have a history of providing reliable returns.
Develop a trading plan: Your trading plan should outline your entry and exit points, as well as your risk management strategies. You should also establish your position sizing, stop loss levels, and profit targets.
Monitor the market: Keeping track of economic news, market trends, and other factors that could affect the value of currency pairs is essential to successful forex trading. You should regularly monitor the market to ensure that your strategy is still relevant and effective.
Regularly review and update your strategy: As the forex market evolves, it is important to review and update your strategy regularly. This will help you stay ahead of the curve and adapt to changing market conditions.

Basic terminologies in forex market

The following are some of the basic terms used in the forex market:

Currency Pair: A currency pair is the quotation and pricing structure of the currencies traded in the forex market. The value of a currency is determined by its comparison to another currency. The first currency of a currency pair is called the base currency, and the second is called the quote currency. For example, in the currency pair EUR/USD, EUR is the base currency, and USD is the quote currency.

Bid Price: The bid price is the highest price that a buyer is willing to pay for a currency. It is the price at which the market maker is willing to buy the base currency.

Ask Price: The ask price is the lowest price that a seller is willing to receive for a currency. It is the price at which the market maker is willing to sell the base currency.

Spread: The spread is the difference between the bid and ask price. It is the cost of trading in the forex market and is usually measured in pips.

Example of currency trading

Here is an example of currency trading, both in terms of loss and profit:

Suppose an investor named John wants to trade in the currency market. He has $100,000, and he wants to convert it into euros (EUR). The current exchange rate between the US dollar (USD) and the euro is 1 USD = 0.8 EUR. John buys 80,000 EUR using his $100,000.

A few days later, the exchange rate becomes 1 USD = 0.7 EUR. Juan decides to convert his euros back into dollars, and sells his 80,000 EUR in exchange for 114,285 USD. In this scenario, John has made a profit of $14,285 because the exchange rate has moved in his favor.

In this scenario, John has made a loss of $12,500 because the exchange rate has moved against him.

Risk in forex trading

Forex trading, like any other type of financial investment, carries various types of risks that traders should be aware of before making a trade. Some of the key risks involved in forex trading include:

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Market Volatility: The forex market is highly volatile, meaning that exchange rates can fluctuate rapidly and unpredictably. This can result in sudden and significant losses for traders who are not prepared for these changes.
Leverage: Forex trading is often leveraged, meaning that traders can control large amounts of currency with a relatively small amount of capital. This leverage can magnify potential gains, but it also magnifies potential losses.
Interest rate risk: The difference in interest rates between two countries can affect the value of their currencies. If a currency has a higher interest rate than another, investors will be attracted to it, causing its value to rise relative to the other currency.
Political and economic instability: Political and economic events can have a major impact on exchange rates. For example, a political crisis in a country can led to a sharp devaluation of its currency.
Liquidity risk: The forex market is highly liquid, but this does not mean that all currencies are equally easy to trade. Some currencies may be thinly traded, making it difficult to find a buyer or seller when you need to close your position.
Currency correlation risk: Currencies are often correlated, meaning they can move in tandem. This can create risks for traders who are not aware of these relationships and who are not properly diversifying their portfolios.
Fraud risk: Unfortunately, forex brokers or traders have been engaging in fraudulent activities, such as “stop-loss hunting” or price manipulation. It’s important to only trade with reputable and regulated brokers.
