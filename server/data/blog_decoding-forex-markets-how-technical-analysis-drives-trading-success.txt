How Do Price Trends Shape Forex Trading?¬†

A fundamental principle of technical analysis is that prices tend to move in trends rather than random patterns. A trend can be upward (bullish), downward (bearish), or sideways (range-bound). Traders use trend analysis to find entry and exit points based on the momentum of the market.

What Are the Most Common Chart Patterns in Forex?¬†

Chart patterns are visual representations of price movements that traders use to forecast potential breakouts or reversals. Several of the most widely recognised patterns include:¬†

Head and Shoulders: Signals a trend reversal, where a peak (head) is flanked by two smaller peaks (shoulders).¬†
Double Tops and Double Bottoms: Show potential reversals at strong resistance (double top) or support (double bottom) levels.¬†
Triangles (Ascending, Descending, and Symmetrical): Suggest a consolidation phase before a breakout in either direction.

Which Technical Indicators Are Most Commonly Used?¬†

Technical indicators provide traders with statistical tools to analyse price movements and market momentum. Some of the most used indicators in Forex trading include:¬†

Moving Averages (MA): Help with smooth price data to find overall trends (e.g., 50-day and 200-day moving averages).¬†
Relative Strength Index (RSI): Measures speed and change of price movements to show overbought or oversold conditions.¬†
MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence): Names trend direction and momentum through moving average crossovers.¬†
Bollinger Bands: Measure market volatility and show overextended price movements.

How Do Support and Resistance Levels Affect Trading Decisions?¬†

Support and resistance levels are key concepts in technical analysis that represent price points where buying or selling pressure tends to emerge.¬†

Support Level: A price level where demand is strong enough to prevent further declines.¬†
Resistance Level: A price level where selling pressure prevents further price increases.

Why Is Market Psychology Important in Technical Analysis?¬†

Market psychology plays a crucial role in Forex trading, as price movements are heavily influenced by traders' emotions, sentiment, and behavioural biases. Fear and greed drive short-term price fluctuations, leading to trends, reversals, and breakouts.
