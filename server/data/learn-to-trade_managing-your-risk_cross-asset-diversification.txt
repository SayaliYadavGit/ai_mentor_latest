Reducing Portfolio Risk:

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Q: What account types does Hantec offer?

The primary objective of cross-asset diversification is to reduce overall portfolio risk by spreading investments across assets with different risk-return profiles. Since different asset classes tend to perform differently under various market conditions, diversifying across them helps mitigate the impact of adverse market movements on the portfolio's value.

Low Correlation Between Assets:

The key to effective cross-asset diversification is investing in assets with low correlations or negative correlations. Correlation measures the degree to which the prices of two assets move in relation to each other. Assets with low correlations tend to move independently of each other, providing diversification benefits. For example, when stocks decline, bonds or gold may appreciate, offsetting losses in the stock portion of the portfolio.

Rebalancing a Diversified Portfolio
What is Portfolio Rebalancing?

Portfolio rebalancing is the process of realigning the asset allocation of your investment portfolio to bring it back in line with your target allocation. Over time, as different asset classes or individual investments perform differently, your portfolio's asset allocation can drift away from your original investment plan. Rebalancing ensures that your portfolio remains consistent with your risk tolerance and investment objectives.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Q: What do I need to know about Forex?

Why is Rebalancing Important?

Rebalancing is essential for several reasons:

Maintaining Asset Allocation: Rebalancing helps ensure that your portfolio maintains the desired mix of assets to achieve your investment goals and risk tolerance.
Risk Control: Market fluctuations can cause certain assets in your portfolio to become overweight or underweight, leading to unintended risk exposure. Rebalancing helps control risk by restoring asset allocations to their target levels.
Maximising Returns: Rebalancing allows you to take advantage of market trends and opportunities by selling well-performing assets and buying undervalued or potential future growth assets.

Steps to Rebalancing:

When rebalancing your portfolio, follow these steps:

Review Your Current Asset Allocation: Assess the current allocation of your portfolio across different asset classes, such as stocks, bonds, cash, and alternative investments.
Compare to Target Allocation: Compare your current asset allocation to your target allocation or investment plan. Identify any deviations or overweights/ underweights in specific asset classes.
Determine Rebalancing Strategy: Decide how you will rebalance your portfolio to align it with your target allocation. This may involve selling overweight assets, buying underweighted assets, or combining both.
Execute Rebalancing Trades: Implement the rebalancing strategy by executing trades to adjust the allocation of assets in your portfolio. When making trades, consider transaction costs, tax implications, and market liquidity.
Monitor and Adjust: Regularly monitor your portfolio’s performance and asset allocation. Adjust your rebalancing strategy as needed based on changes in market conditions, investment goals, or risk tolerance.
Considerations for Rebalancing a Diversified Portfolio:
Tax Implications: Rebalancing can trigger capital gains taxes, especially in taxable investment accounts. Consider tax-efficient strategies such as tax-loss harvesting or using tax-advantaged accounts like IRAs and 401(k)s.
Transaction Costs: Be mindful of transaction costs associated with buying and selling securities. Minimise trading expenses using low-cost investment vehicles such as index funds or ETFs.
Market Conditions: Rebalance your portfolio in light of prevailing market conditions and economic outlook. Adjust your asset allocation and investment strategy to reflect changes in interest rates, inflation expectations, and geopolitical risks.

Position Sizing and Risk Allocation:

Proper position sizing is crucial for managing risk. Avoid overexposing your portfolio to any single asset or market. Allocate capital based on each asset's risk-return profile, considering factors such as volatility, liquidity, and correlation with other portfolio holdings. Every investment involves a trade-off between risk and reward. Higher potential returns typically come with higher levels of risk. Use of Stop-Loss Orders:

Implementing stop-loss orders is a proactive risk management strategy. A stop-loss order specifies a price at which a trade will be automatically exited to limit losses. Setting stop-loss orders can protect your capital and prevent emotional decision-making during market volatility.
