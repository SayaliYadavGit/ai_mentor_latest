You should choose a course that allows you to practise what you've learnt in some kind of real-world scenario. Flexibility and Learning Format

The way you prefer to learn and the format in which you're comfortable can significantly impact your success in a trading course. Consider these factors when evaluating different options:

Online vs. In-Person: When it comes to format, think about your preference between online and in-person courses. Online courses offer flexibility, enabling you to learn from the comfort of your home, while in-person classes provide the advantage of direct interaction with instructors and peers. Opt for the format that aligns best with your lifestyle and personal learning style.

Self-Paced vs. Structured: Courses come in two main formats: self-paced and structured. Self-paced courses let you learn at your own speed, while structured ones have set timelines and deadlines. Consider your own discipline and focus and choose the format that suits your learning style and pace. If you thrive with self-discipline, a self-paced course might be ideal.
